## **KEY THEMES ACROSS ALL YEARS**

1. **Chronological Understanding**: Building sense of time from earliest to modern
2. **Cause and Effect**: Understanding why things happened
3. **Change and Continuity**: How things changed and what stayed the same
4. **Historical Significance**: Why certain events and people matter
5. **Evidence and Sources**: How we know about the past
6. **Perspectives**: Different viewpoints on historical events

---

## **ASSESSMENT APPROACH**

- **Nursery & Reception**: Observation, discussion, simple activities
- **Year 1-2**: Quizzes, simple projects, drawings
- **Year 3-4**: Quizzes, projects, written work, timelines
- **Year 5-6**: Quizzes, tests, research projects, essays, source analysis

---

## **RESOURCES NEEDED**

- Visual timelines
- Maps (historical and modern)
- Primary source documents (age-appropriate)
- Artifacts and replicas
- Books and stories
- Videos and documentaries
- Museum visits (virtual or real)
- Art supplies for projects
- Technology for research

---

## **IMPLEMENTATION NOTES**

1. **Adaptability**: Lessons can be adapted based on student interest and local curriculum requirements
2. **Integration**: History lessons can integrate with other subjects (geography, English, art, science)
3. **Sensitivity**: Some topics (wars, difficult periods) should be handled age-appropriately
4. **Diversity**: Ensure representation of different cultures and perspectives
5. **Active Learning**: Include hands-on activities, role-play, and projects
6. **Progression**: Each year builds on previous knowledge while introducing new concepts

---

This curriculum provides a comprehensive journey through world history from the age of dinosaurs through biblical times, ancient civilizations, medieval periods, and modern history, all tailored to be age-appropriate and engaging for primary school students.
